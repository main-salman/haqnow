# 📂 High-Profile Leaked & Declassified Documents for HaqNow.org

A curated list of publicly available, verified document collections that could be downloaded and uploaded to HaqNow.org. Each entry includes direct links to official or authoritative sources where the documents can be accessed.

> **Note:** All documents listed here are already in the public domain — released by governments, courts, investigative journalists, or official archives. Priority is given to collections with downloadable PDFs.

## 🔴 Tier 1 — Highest Profile (Immediate Impact)

### 1. Jeffrey Epstein Files
- **What:** Court records, DOJ disclosures, FBI documents, FOIA releases, and House Oversight Committee materials related to Jeffrey Epstein's sex trafficking network. Millions of pages including indictments, motions, exhibits, emails, and photographs.
- **Source:** U.S. Department of Justice (mandated by the Epstein Files Transparency Act, signed Nov 2025)
- **Link:** [justice.gov/epstein](https://www.justice.gov/epstein)
- **Format:** Searchable PDFs, organized by category
- **Status:** ✅ Fully public, official government source

### 2. JFK Assassination Records
- **What:** 6+ million pages of records related to the 1963 assassination, including CIA, FBI, and Secret Service documents. Covers the assassinations of JFK, RFK, and MLK Jr.
- **Source:** National Archives and Records Administration (NARA), per Executive Order 14176 (Jan 2025)
- **Link:** [archives.gov/research/jfk](https://www.archives.gov/research/jfk)
- **Format:** Digitized PDFs
- **Status:** ✅ Fully public, official government source

### 3. CIA Torture Report (Senate Intelligence Committee)
- **What:** 525-page executive summary of the Committee Study of the CIA's Detention and Interrogation Program. Documents "enhanced interrogation techniques" (torture) used at CIA black sites post-9/11.
- **Source:** Senate Select Committee on Intelligence (declassified Dec 2014)
- **Links:**
  - Executive Summary: Search [intelligence.senate.gov](https://www.intelligence.senate.gov) for "SSCI Study"
  - Related FOIA documents: [aclu.org/issues/national-security/torture](https://www.aclu.org/issues/national-security/torture)
  - "CIA Torture Unredacted" compilation: [therenditionproject.org.uk](https://www.therenditionproject.org.uk)
- **Format:** PDF
- **Status:** ✅ Executive summary public; full report still classified

### 4. Pentagon Papers
- **What:** ~7,000 pages — "Report of the Office of the Secretary of Defense Vietnam Task Force." Exposed systematic government deception about the Vietnam War spanning multiple administrations.
- **Source:** National Archives (fully declassified 2011)
- **Link:** [catalog.archives.gov](https://catalog.archives.gov/) — Search for National Archives Identifier **5890484**
- **Format:** Large PDFs (save to disk before opening)
- **Status:** ✅ Fully public, fully declassified, official source

## 🟠 Tier 2 — Major International Investigations

### 5. ICIJ Offshore Leaks Database (Panama Papers, Pandora Papers, Paradise Papers)
- **What:** Structured data from multiple major financial leak investigations exposing offshore tax havens and shell companies used by world leaders, billionaires, and corporations.
  - **Panama Papers** (2016): 11.5M documents from Mossack Fonseca
  - **Paradise Papers** (2017): 13.4M documents from Appleby
  - **Pandora Papers** (2021): 11.9M documents from 14 offshore service providers
- **Source:** International Consortium of Investigative Journalists (ICIJ)
- **Links:**
  - Searchable database: [offshoreleaks.icij.org](https://offshoreleaks.icij.org/)
  - Bulk data download (CSV/Neo4j): [offshoreleaks.icij.org/pages/database](https://offshoreleaks.icij.org/pages/database)
- **Format:** CSV files, Neo4j database dumps
- **Note:** Raw source documents (emails, passports) are NOT publicly released — only structured entity data
- **Status:** ✅ Structured data public; raw documents restricted

### 6. Xinjiang Police Files
- **What:** Leaked internal Chinese police files from Uyghur detention camps, including detainee photos, internal speeches by officials, security protocols, and shooting orders. Provides direct evidence of the mass internment system.
- **Source:** Leaked to Dr. Adrian Zenz, published May 2022
- **Link:** [xinjiangpolicefiles.org](https://www.xinjiangpolicefiles.org)
- **Format:** PDFs, images
- **Status:** ✅ Fully public

### 7. The Drone Papers
- **What:** Classified Pentagon documents revealing the inner workings of the U.S. drone assassination program (2011–2013). Includes kill list targeting processes, civilian casualty data, and the labeling of all strike deaths as "Enemy Killed in Action."
- **Source:** Anonymous whistleblower via The Intercept (Oct 2015)
- **Link:** [theintercept.com/drone-papers](https://theintercept.com/drone-papers/)
- **Format:** PDFs (Pentagon slide decks embedded in articles)
- **Status:** ✅ Fully public

### 8. 9/11 — The "28 Pages" (Joint Inquiry, Part IV)
- **What:** The classified final chapter of the 2002 Joint Congressional Inquiry into 9/11, detailing investigative leads regarding potential connections between the hijackers and Saudi Arabian government officials.
- **Source:** House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence (declassified July 2016)
- **Links:**
  - [intelligence.house.gov](https://intelligence.house.gov) — Search for "28 pages declassified"
  - Also archived at [digital.library.unt.edu](https://digital.library.unt.edu)
- **Format:** PDF
- **Status:** ✅ Fully public, officially declassified

## 🟡 Tier 3 — Surveillance, Intelligence & Civil Liberties

### 9. Snowden NSA Surveillance Archive
- **What:** Classified NSA documents leaked by Edward Snowden revealing global mass surveillance programs (PRISM, XKeyscore, Tempora, etc.), domestic phone metadata collection, and surveillance of allied heads of state.
- **Source:** Edward Snowden via journalists Glenn Greenwald, Laura Poitras, and others (2013)
- **Links:**
  - Internet Archive collection: [archive.org/details/nsa-snowden-documents](https://archive.org/details/nsa-snowden-documents)
  - Canadian Journalists for Free Expression archive: [snowdenarchive.cjfe.org](https://snowdenarchive.cjfe.org/)
  - GitHub mirror: [github.com/iamcryptoki/snowden-archive](https://github.com/iamcryptoki/snowden-archive)
- **Format:** PDFs, images
- **Status:** ✅ Widely archived and public

### 10. COINTELPRO Documents (FBI)
- **What:** Declassified FBI records on the Counter Intelligence Program — a covert campaign (1956–1971) to surveil, infiltrate, discredit, and disrupt domestic political organizations including civil rights groups, the Black Panther Party, anti-war movements, and others.
- **Source:** FBI (declassified following Church Committee investigations)
- **Link:** [vault.fbi.gov](https://vault.fbi.gov/) — Search for "COINTELPRO"
- **Format:** Scanned PDFs
- **Status:** ✅ Fully public, official FBI source

### 11. CIA MKUltra Documents
- **What:** Surviving declassified records of the CIA's illegal mind control program (1953–1973), which involved experiments on unwitting human subjects using drugs (LSD), hypnosis, and other methods. Most files were destroyed in 1973; a cache was discovered in 1977.
- **Source:** CIA FOIA Electronic Reading Room
- **Links:**
  - Official CIA release: [foia.cia.gov](https://www.foia.cia.gov) — Search "MKULTRA"
  - The Black Vault (organized archive): [theblackvault.com](https://www.theblackvault.com)
- **Format:** Scanned PDFs
- **Status:** ✅ Fully public via FOIA

### 12. Church Committee Reports
- **What:** 14 final reports from the Senate Select Committee to Study Governmental Operations with Respect to Intelligence Activities (1975–1976). Documented illegal intelligence activities including assassination plots against foreign leaders, domestic surveillance, and CIA/FBI abuses.
- **Source:** U.S. Senate
- **Link:** [aarclibrary.org](https://aarclibrary.org) — Full digitized PDFs of all 14 reports
- **Format:** PDFs
- **Status:** ✅ Fully public

## 🔵 Tier 4 — War Crimes & Military Documents

### 13. Guantánamo Bay Detainee Files ("Gitmo Files")
- **What:** 765 classified "Detainee Assessment Briefs" from the Pentagon's Joint Task Force Guantanamo (2002–2009). Reveal that many detainees were held for years without charge, including individuals later deemed low-risk or innocent. Documents how information was extracted through mistreatment.

- **Link:** [wikileaks.org](https://wikileaks.org) — Search "Guantanamo files"
- **Format:** PDFs
- **Status:** ✅ Widely public

### 14. Iraq War Logs

- **Source:** WikiLeaks (Oct 2010)

- **Format:** Database/CSV/PDFs
- **Status:** ✅ Widely public

### 15. Afghanistan War Diary
- **What:** 91,000+ U.S. military incident reports from the Afghanistan War (2004–2009). Expose unreported civilian casualties, friendly fire incidents, and the scope of Taliban operations.
- **Source:** WikiLeaks (July 2010)
- **Link:** [wikileaks.org](https://wikileaks.org) — Afghan War Diary section; also archived on [archive.org](https://archive.org)
- **Format:** Database/CSV
- **Status:** ✅ Widely public

### 16. Abu Ghraib & Torture Archive
- **What:** Tens of thousands of pages of declassified government documents on U.S. detention and interrogation practices, including the "Torture Memos" that provided legal justification for enhanced interrogation.
- **Source:** National Security Archive at George Washington University (compiled from FOIA releases)

## ⚠️ Important Notes

1. **All documents listed are already publicly available** from official government sources, established news organizations, or recognized investigative journalism outlets.
2. **Verify before uploading** — always download from the authoritative source listed and verify file integrity.
3. **Redactions should be preserved** — do not attempt to de-redact any documents. Redactions exist to protect victims and ongoing investigations.
4. **Provide context** — each upload to HaqNow should include a description explaining the document's origin, significance, and source link for verification.
5. **Some collections are very large** (millions of pages) — consider uploading key/representative documents first rather than entire archives.
